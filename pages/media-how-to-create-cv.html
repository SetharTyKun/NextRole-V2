How to Create a Strong CV

JP

Jon Putnam

Fullstack Developer at NextRole

Your CV is often the very first impression recruiters have about you. A well-structured and ATS-friendly CV can dramatically increase your chances of landing interviews and standing out from hundreds of applicants.

Many candidates make the mistake of over-designing their resumes with excessive graphics, icons, and complicated layouts. While visually appealing, these designs can confuse Applicant Tracking Systems and reduce your visibility.

Keep Your Layout Clean

Use clear headings, consistent spacing, and readable typography. Recruiters typically scan resumes quickly, so readability matters more than decorative elements.

Pro Tip

Tailor your CV for every application by matching keywords directly from the job description.

Highlight Your Achievements

Rather than just listing your responsibilities, focus on measurable achievements. Use numbers and results where possible — for example, "Increased sales by 30%" or "Managed a team of 8 engineers."

This approach helps recruiters quickly understand the real impact you've had in your previous roles and gives you a competitive edge over candidates who only list duties.

Keep It Concise

Most recruiters spend fewer than 10 seconds on an initial CV scan. Aim for one to two pages maximum. Include only the most relevant experiences and tailor each version to the specific role you're applying for.

Avoid lengthy paragraphs. Use bullet points to make your CV easy to skim, and lead with your strongest points at the top of each section.

Pro Tip

Run your CV through a free ATS checker before submitting — many online tools can flag formatting issues that might get you filtered out automatically.
